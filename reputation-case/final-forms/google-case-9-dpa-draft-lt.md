# Draft Complaint to Lithuanian DPA (VDAI) - if Google maintains refusal

Subject: Complaint regarding Google LLC decision under EU data protection delisting request (Case 9-6257000039625)

I, Ilia Klishin, submit this complaint regarding Google's decision in case 9-6257000039625.

Summary:
- I requested delisting of specific URLs for direct searches of my name due to inaccurate, misleading, context-deficient, and disproportionate reputational labeling.
- Google partially responded that some URLs are not currently displayed/removed, and declined action for other URLs citing public-interest/professional-life grounds.

Why I challenge the refusal:
1. The declined URLs use severe categorical labels as settled identity facts in direct person-name search contexts.
2. The resulting impression is disproportionate relative to current, contextualized, and contested factual record.
3. The labeling is driven in part by a decontextualized interpretation of a historical oral and hypothetical Crimea-related response that was later published in edited column format without adequate context.
4. The underlying hypothetical was conditional (a negotiated scenario only if both Russia and Ukraine joined NATO and the EU), yet is often flattened into a categorical identity label.
5. This omits material counter-context: in 2014, while serving as editor-in-chief of TV Rain website, I worked on the "Nashi Soldaty" investigation about Russian soldiers killed/captured in Donbas during official denials.
6. Therefore, name-based search display produces a distorted and disproportionately harmful first impression.
7. The request seeks name-based delisting in the EU context, not global removal of information from the web.
8. Ongoing discoverability causes continuing reputational harm.

Requested supervisory action:
- Review Google's balancing test in this case.
- Require reassessment of declined URLs for name-based delisting.
