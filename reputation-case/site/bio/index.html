Biography

Ilia Klishin

Also published as Ilya Klishin · Илья Клишин.

Ilia Klishin is a Russian journalist, media manager, and media consultant based in Vilnius. His work connects editorial leadership, media strategy, public analysis, and projects for Russian-speaking audiences in Europe.

He was editor-in-chief of the website of TV Rain (Dozhd), the independent Russian television channel, and later led digital editorial work at RTVI during the channel’s relaunch. Today he works in media consulting and develops independent editorial and community projects.

His main themes are Russian digital propaganda, pressure on independent media, Russian politics, exile, public language, and civic infrastructure.

Professional profile

Born in 1987, Ilia Klishin has worked across journalism, digital editorial leadership, media management, and consulting. His public work focuses on Russian politics, digital propaganda, independent media, and the political and cultural experience of Russian-speaking exile.

Current work

Klishin is co-founder of KF Agency, a media consulting practice focused on editorial systems, audience strategy, distribution, and digital media development. He is also the founder of Volna, a Russian-language media project for relocated communities in Europe, and runs literary and community projects including ReadMe.txt and book clubs.

Past editorial roles

From 2013 to 2016, Klishin was editor-in-chief of the website of TV Rain (Dozhd). From 2016 to 2019, he led digital editorial work at RTVI during the channel’s relaunch.

Research and analysis

His analysis focuses on Russian digital propaganda, platform power, information warfare, protest politics, independent media, emigrant media, and the politics of public language.

Selected projects

Selected projects include Volna, KF Agency, ReadMe.txt, book clubs, public commentary, and editorial consulting work for media, cultural, and civic projects.

Consulting and collaboration

His consulting work covers editorial audits, audience development, distribution architecture, content strategy, newsroom processes, and practical media-product development.
