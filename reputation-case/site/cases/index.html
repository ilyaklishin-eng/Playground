Case 1

Fair Elections rally relocation: Revolution Square to Bolotnaya Square

My role in this episode was operational, not decision-making. I administered the Facebook page used to coordinate participant updates, but I was not the person who decided to move the rally.

The relocation from Revolution Square to Bolotnaya Square was a collective decision of the full organizing committee following talks with the city authorities and the presentation of security assurances. The timeline is important: the final decision was taken roughly 36 hours before the rally. In that situation, distributing parallel messages about two possible locations would have risked confusion, fragmented routes, delayed arrivals, and unnecessary crowd-safety problems.

My task was to execute the committee's decision responsibly: provide one clear location update, keep the messaging consistent, and reduce conflicting instructions for participants. The underlying decision was made collectively on the basis of the information available to the organizers at the time.

Case 2

TV Rain and the blockade-Leningrad poll controversy (January 2014)

This episode took place in late January 2014, at a time when TV Rain was already under growing political pressure in Russia. As early as December 2013, people close to the Presidential Administration had signaled that the authorities were looking for a pretext to move against independent media, including TV Rain.

The controversial blockade-Leningrad poll, which appeared during the partner historical program Diletanty, should therefore be understood not as the true cause of the crackdown, but as a convenient trigger. One important indication of this was the speed and scale of the reaction: within a very short period on a Sunday evening, a large number of pro-government bloggers and media outlets launched a synchronized attack on the channel in strikingly similar terms. That pattern makes a purely spontaneous reaction unlikely and suggests that the infrastructure for such a campaign had already been prepared.

In that sense, the poll exposed a vulnerability, but it did not create the political will to destroy the channel. It merely provided an opportunity to execute a broader scenario that appears to have been ready in advance.

Case 3

The 2014 Snob publication and the Current Time hiring controversy (2023)

The Snob text, published in April 2014, before the start of active combat operations in Donbas, was neither a programmatic article nor a political manifesto. It was a response to a hypothetical question framed in explicitly conditional terms. My remark referred to an imagined scenario and included speculative parallels, such as Serbia and Kosovo, or a future in which both Russia and Ukraine might simultaneously join the EU and NATO.

I do not deny that I said this. At the same time, I acknowledge that the remark was overly abstract and, in retrospect, insensitive. At the time, I underestimated the risks of such sophistry. That is why I later asked the editors to remove the text.

My actual editorial position in 2014 is better reflected by my work than by that isolated theoretical comment. Under my leadership, the TV Rain website editorial team became the first newsroom in Russia to publish a project documenting Russian soldiers killed in Donbas, at a time when the Russian state publicly denied their presence there.

In 2023, the old Snob comment resurfaced during discussions at Current Time about appointing a single manager to oversee both the Russian and Ukrainian editorial teams without formally merging them. I was not aware of the internal tensions surrounding that process and agreed only to consider the role and begin preliminary conversations. In that environment, members of the Ukrainian newsroom made clear that any Russian candidate for such a position would be subjected to intense scrutiny and that past statements would be searched for and used as grounds for opposition. The 2014 comment was then pulled from the archive, stripped of its original context, and weaponized publicly in a way that made my appointment impossible, even though I had neither sought nor lobbied for the position.
