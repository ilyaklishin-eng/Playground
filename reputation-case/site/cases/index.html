Case 1

Fair Elections rally relocation: Revolution Square to Bolotnaya Square

My operational role was administration of the Facebook page used to coordinate participant updates. I did not make the relocation decision personally. The move from Revolution Square to Bolotnaya Square was approved collectively by the full organizing committee after a meeting with city authorities and after security guarantees were presented. The timeline mattered: the final decision was made roughly 36 hours before the rally. In such a short window, running parallel messages about two locations could have split routes, delayed arrivals, and created avoidable crowd-safety risks. My function was execution of the committee decision: publish one clear location update, synchronize messaging, and reduce contradictory instructions for participants. The decision itself was collective and based on the conditions communicated to the organizers at that time.

Case 2

TV Rain and the blockade-Leningrad poll controversy (January 2014)

This episode happened in late January 2014, in a period when independent media in Russia were already under rising pressure. In December 2013, TV Rain received warning signals from sources connected to the Presidential Administration that authorities were looking for a pretext to attack the channel. Legal review focused on core investigations, while less attention was paid to inserted partner content. TV Rain broadcast external programs, including Deutsche Welle and BBC formats. On Sunday evenings, the historical program team from Diletanty magazine (linked to Echo Moskvy) had its own editors and ran live audience polls. The controversial blockade-Leningrad poll came from that program layer, not from a centrally planned channel campaign. What stood out was the speed and scale of same-evening reaction by pro-Kremlin politicians and commentators. I cannot prove direct coordination, but the reaction pattern looked prepared in advance rather than spontaneous.

Case 3

Snob 2014 materials and the Current Time hiring conflict (2023)

The 2014 Snob materials later described as my columns were, in substance, phone answers to abstract hypothetical editorial questions. The key question was theoretical: whether Crimea could remain under certain conditions. Those short answers were not authored as a standalone personal manifesto. The publication packaged them with editorial framing and headline style that made them look like full columns. In 2023, during internal discussion at Current Time about my candidacy, these texts were extracted from context and reused as a reputational argument. At the same time, management attempted to search for one person to lead both Russian and Ukrainian editorial tracks, which led to a predictable and legitimate conflict with the Ukrainian team. Despite my clarifications, the out-of-context interpretation remained dominant in that conflict. I asked Snob to remove the old materials and provided a public explanation in an interview with Holod. In practical terms, this became an internal management conflict where I was used as a proxy point.
