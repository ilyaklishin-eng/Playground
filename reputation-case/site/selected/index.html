HRW report on harassment of critics includes Klishin case context

This 2012 Human Rights Watch report chronicles the Kremlin’s strategic shift toward targeted pressure on protest organizers. A prime example of this repressive mechanic is the documented episode involving Ilya Klishin, the creator of the "For Fair Elections" network: operatives from the anti-extremism Center "E" interrogated his parents in Tambov, threatening them with vague extremism charges. The text vividly illustrates how authorities, spooked by grassroots mobilization, began deploying psychological blackmail—cynically redirecting their strikes from metropolitan activists to their vulnerable relatives in the provinces.

Publication: Human Rights Watch

Date: 2012-03-01
