Interview on creating an emigrant-focused media project

7x7 | 2023-09-27 | EN | emigration media

Summary

This Sept. 27, 2023 interview in 7x7 documents the launch logic and editorial method behind the Russian-language "Volna" project in Lithuania. Klishin describes the startup phase as fast and low-bureaucracy: no grant-first cycle, immediate publishing, and iterative format decisions based on user utility. The core product is a daily digest that translates local institutional and civic information into accessible Russian for audiences newly arrived in the country. The interview emphasizes why this format was chosen over higher-volume posting: newcomers need orientation, legal-context clarity, and calm verification more than engagement-maximizing frequency. It also addresses audience composition across Russian, Belarusian, Ukrainian, and local communities, presenting balance as an active editorial task rather than a fixed input. In crisis moments, the team prioritizes speed with document-level checking to avoid panic amplification. As a source, the interview is useful for understanding exile-media operations: product design, trust-building routines, and multilingual diaspora service under rapid migration and policy change conditions.

Key Ideas

  • Volna started with rapid execution, not grant-dependent preproduction cycles.
  • Daily digest format prioritized orientation utility over algorithmic post volume.
  • Editorial balance across diaspora groups requires intentional local community work.
