TV Rain launched "Our Soldiers" civilian investigation

TV Rain | 2014-08-27 | EN | war-reporting verification

Summary

This August 2014 TV Rain project page introduces "Our Soldiers," a public-facing investigation into reports of Russian servicemen killed, missing, or captured near the Ukrainian border during a period of official denials. The framing is explicit: the goal is not rhetorical escalation but factual clarification of identities, numbers, and circumstances. The page states that if authorities withhold information, independent verification must be built from open submissions, witness reports, and case-level documentation. It asks readers to send evidence about specific soldiers and positions the project as a civic-journalistic archive rather than a one-off article. In practical terms, this format matters because it converts scattered signals into a trackable reporting workflow: intake, verification, publication, and correction potential. For reputation and historical indexing, the source is significant as contemporaneous newsroom evidence of anti-obfuscation reporting in 2014, when discussing Russian military presence in Donbas carried high political risk. It documents a concrete investigative initiative with public methodology and clear evidentiary intent.

Key Ideas

  • Project design focused on verifiable case records, not slogan-driven coverage.
  • Audience submissions were used to expand evidence on named servicemen.
  • The page captures independent wartime verification practice in real time.
