Holod interview with Ilya Klishin

Holod | 2023-07-13 | EN | long-form interview

Summary

This July 13, 2023 interview in Holod gives an operational view of how the Volna media project was launched and scaled for Russian-speaking relocants in Lithuania, Latvia, Germany, and other destinations. Klishin describes the product logic as service-first: daily local digests, practical orientation, and lower-anxiety information flow for people adapting to new legal and social environments. The conversation explains why Volna did not copy high-frequency conflict feeds from major exile outlets; instead, it prioritized usable local context, from transport and documents to civic events, while keeping an explicit anti-war position. The interview also discusses editorial boundaries in host countries, including a rule against imposing outsider opinions on sensitive domestic policy disputes. Business sustainability is presented as mixed-model: donations, Patreon, advertising, and partnerships rather than single-source dependency. The text is useful because it combines strategic framing with concrete newsroom mechanics: audience definition, editorial ratio choices, expansion criteria, and governance practices in a diaspora-media setting during wartime displacement.

Key Ideas

  • Volna was designed as an adaptation tool for relocated audiences.
  • Editorial utility and calm verification outweighed engagement-maximizing posting volume.
  • Revenue diversification supported independence from one funding pipeline.
